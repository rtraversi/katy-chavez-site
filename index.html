Traffic & DWI

Protecting your license, your insurance rates, and your record in Wake County courts.

Traffic Tickets

I can handle your traffic tickets received in Wake County — you won't need to come to court. If you mail in your ticket with payment, you are pleading guilty and your insurance will definitely go up. The minimum increase is 25% per year for three years.

In Wake County, speeding tickets can often be reduced to a non-moving violation of Improper Equipment — meaning no insurance points or license points, ever. If you don't qualify, I may seek a PJC. If your ticket came from an accident, I may be able to get it dismissed entirely. Tickets for no driver's license can be dismissed if you get your license before court date.

Already made the mistake of not hiring an attorney on a prior ticket and your insurance has gone up? We may still be able to file a motion to reopen the case and ask for a reduction.

DWLR — Driving While License Revoked

This is one of the most important traffic charges to have an attorney for. The rules are complicated and the consequences are serious — including potential jail time and further revocations. Third-time revocations can be permanent. I can help you understand your options and resolve underlying charges efficiently.

DWI

I will order the officer's reports, the chemical analyst's notes, interview the officer, and develop a trial strategy. There are often strong legal grounds to challenge a DWI charge — from the legality of the initial stop to the validity of the breath test results.

Getting a Driver's License

If you're having trouble getting a license due to ID requirements, unresolved tickets, or other issues, I can help you work through those barriers. Remember: only the DMV can issue a valid ID or driver's license — never attempt to use a store-bought ID.

Frequently Asked Questions — DWLR

Can I get a limited driving privilege if my license is revoked? ▼
Maybe. There are limited privileges available for some revocations. It may also be possible to take care of outstanding charges, re-open old charges, or request a special DMV hearing to get your license back.
If I pay all my old tickets, will I be able to get my license back? ▼
Probably not. Paying old tickets can often cause more problems than it solves — especially if those tickets were received while you were already revoked. Always ask an attorney before paying old tickets.
If I never had a license, how can I be charged with DWLR? ▼
Not having a license doesn't avoid revocation. If you get a ticket and don't take care of it, your driving privilege becomes revoked. Driving while revoked can result in jail and further revocations — much more serious than Driving Without a License.
If I was revoked for DWI more than a year ago, why is my license still revoked? ▼
Many people fail to complete required alcohol treatment within 12 months of conviction. Until treatment is complete, the license stays revoked — and there is no Limited Driving Privilege for this type of revocation. It can also take 2+ months after completing treatment before the DMV processes it.

Frequently Asked Questions — DWI

What is the punishment for DWI? ▼
There are five levels of punishment. Without a prior conviction, child in the car, or accident, you'll likely receive Level 3, 4, or 5 — which involves a fine, license revocation, required alcohol treatment, and a suspended (not active) jail sentence. Insurance will increase by 400% or more.
What can I do to lessen my sentence? ▼
After the offense date, the most impactful thing you can do is go to an alcohol treatment facility for an evaluation. Other sentencing factors include whether there was an accident, the alcohol reading level, and your driving record.
If I was actually drinking and driving, is there any point to fighting the charge? ▼
Yes. There are many ways to challenge the case — including the validity of the Intoxilyzer results, whether the officer had legal grounds to stop you, or whether you were actually impaired. Email me to discuss your specific situation.
How do I get a Limited Driving Privilege? ▼
If you had a valid license at the time of offense, you may be eligible for a limited privilege 10 days after the offense and after any conviction. You'll need to have completed an alcohol assessment and be currently insured.

Criminal Defense

I handle criminal charges in Wake County only — focused representation so I can do the best job possible for you.

Sentencing

The most common question I'm asked is: What will my punishment be? The honest answer is: it depends — mostly on prior convictions. For misdemeanors, prior history is actually more important than the nature of the crime.

A person with a clean record (Level I) generally will not face jail for most misdemeanors. Only Class A1 misdemeanors — such as Stalking, Assault on a Female or Child, Assault with a Deadly Weapon, or Violation of a Protective Order — may result in jail time for a first offender. On the other hand, even a minor charge can mean jail time for someone with a long record.

Assault on a Female / Domestic Violence

Domestic violence cases follow strict rules — the prosecutor has little discretion to dismiss these charges even if the victim wants to drop them. However, the State often offers a deferral program where the Defendant can earn a dismissal by completing anger management, alcohol treatment, or family counseling.

Important: If you are not a US Citizen, these programs are treated differently by immigration than by the courts. Please consult with me about immigration implications before making any decisions.

Simple Assault / Communicating a Threat

Wake County gives parties the opportunity to resolve these charges through Mediation, where volunteer counselors help parties work through their issues and agree to solutions. A successful Mediation contract can result in the formal charges being dismissed.

Larceny / Shoplifting

First offenses can be dismissed after completing community service through the First Offender's Program. Otherwise a fine is usually sufficient. Subsequent offenses carry progressively harsher penalties. We can also challenge the charge directly and ask the State to produce witnesses.

Drug Charges

There is a special program for first-time drug possession charges where you can earn a dismissal by completing classes and community service. The time commitment varies by drug type. Possession of cocaine or heroin is a felony in North Carolina. As with larceny, punishments increase significantly with each subsequent offense. There may also be defenses based on the legality of the stop or search.

Resisting or Delaying an Officer

These charges carry their own considerations and are best discussed in a direct consultation. Please reach out and I'll walk you through your options.

Immigration Services

Family petitions, naturalization, TPS renewals, green card renewals, and provisional waivers (I-601a).

What I Handle

If you are a Permanent Resident Alien or US Citizen wanting to petition for a family member, I can help. I also assist with:

  • Applications for Naturalization
  • TPS (Temporary Protected Status) renewals
  • Green Card renewals
  • I-601a Provisional Unlawful Presence Waivers
  • Family-based petitions for spouses of US Citizens

I do not handle employment-based petitions or removal/deportation defense, but I can refer you to excellent attorneys who do.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a Legal Permanent Resident apply for parents or siblings? ▼
No. You must be a US Citizen to petition for parents or siblings.
I'm a US Citizen married to an undocumented immigrant. Can I apply for them? ▼
Maybe. If your spouse entered with any type of visa, Border Crosser card, or as a Canadian, they may be able to adjust status without leaving the country — even if they overstayed. If they entered without inspection, they will generally need to leave the country and apply at a consulate. If they've been out of status for more than one year, a hardship waiver (I-601a) will be required before they can return. Contact me to discuss your specific situation.
Can I get a green card through my US-born children? ▼
Not until they are 21 years old. Once they are 21, they can petition for you if you entered the US legally (with inspection).
What is the I-601a Provisional Waiver? ▼
The I-601a is a waiver of bars to admissibility caused by unlawful presence. It allows someone who would otherwise be barred from returning to the US to apply for the bar to be waived. The waiver is based on hardship to a qualifying US Citizen spouse or parent. The applicant can remain in the US while the waiver is being decided. If approved, they would then leave the country briefly to receive their residency at a consulate and return legally.
How is the I-601a waiver decision made? ▼
The decision is based on proving exceptional and extreme hardship to a qualifying US Citizen relative if the alien could not return. The letter and supporting evidence must show both why the US Citizen cannot simply live in the foreign country, and why living apart in the US would also cause extreme hardship. Cases prepared by an attorney have a significantly higher approval rate.
Does my spouse need to leave the US while the I-601a is being decided? ▼
No. For cases where the only bar is unlawful presence, the applicant can remain in the United States until the waiver is approved. They will only need to leave for a few business days to receive their residency at the consulate.
My spouse entered legally on a visa. How do I apply for their residency? ▼
If your spouse entered with any type of inspection (visa, VWP, Border Crosser card, or as a Canadian), they can likely Adjust Status without leaving the country — even if they've overstayed for many years. I will need birth certificates for both of you, marriage certificate, proof of income and tax forms for the last 3 years, any prior divorces, photos, and copies of the immigrant's passport and visa.
